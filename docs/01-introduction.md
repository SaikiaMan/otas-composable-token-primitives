# Introduction

Distributed ledger token standards have enabled programmable ownership and digital asset transfer across blockchain ecosystems. However, current token standards remain primarily ownership-centric and do not sufficiently address institutional post-trade requirements such as compliance enforcement, identity-aware transfers, interoperable settlement coordination, and standardized metadata propagation.

As tokenized financial systems evolve toward institutional adoption, fragmented token implementations across heterogeneous distributed ledger systems introduce operational, regulatory, and interoperability challenges.

This research explores composable token primitives as a modular architectural model capable of extending token systems through reusable primitives for:

- ownership
- identity
- compliance
- metadata
- settlement coordination

The proposed approach investigates how modular token primitives may improve interoperability and standardization across post-trade financial infrastructures.
