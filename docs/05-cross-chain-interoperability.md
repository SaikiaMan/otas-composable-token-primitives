# Cross-Chain Interoperability

Current token standards remain tightly coupled to specific execution environments and interoperability assumptions.

Cross-chain interoperability systems such as bridges, intent protocols, and settlement coordination frameworks improve portability between distributed ledger systems but introduce fragmented trust assumptions, inconsistent metadata propagation, and heterogeneous execution guarantees.

**Key Observations**

- ERC-7683 demonstrates how intent-based architectures may improve interoperable settlement coordination through standardized order and execution interfaces.
- Interoperability standards alone do not solve identity enforcement, compliance validation, or metadata standardization challenges.

**Role of Composable Primitives**

Composable token primitives may complement interoperability standards by enabling portable compliance, identity, and settlement behaviors across heterogeneous ecosystems.
